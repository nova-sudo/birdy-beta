# Playbook: Scaling without breaking CPL

**Reach for it when:** you've found a genuine winner (healthy CPL *and* quality) and want to grow it
without wrecking it. Scaling is where good accounts get broken.

## Principles

- **Scale the ad set/campaign, not the ad.** Budget lives at the ad set (ABO) or campaign (CBO).
- **~20–30% budget increases, then let it settle** (a few days). Big jumps re-trigger the learning
  phase and spike CPL.
- **Scale into headroom, not into a wall.** A winner with rising frequency has little audience left —
  scaling it just accelerates fatigue (`fatigue.md`). Broaden the audience or duplicate into a new one.
- **Horizontal > vertical when tapped out.** New audiences/creatives (horizontal) often beat forcing
  more budget through a saturating ad set (vertical).
- **Protect the learning.** Under CBO the campaign reallocates for you — hard-capping ad-set budgets
  fights the algorithm.

## Before you scale, confirm

1. Consistently at/below target CPL (not a one-day fluke),
2. Acceptable lead quality (leads become opportunities / connect on calls — `lead-quality.md`),
3. Frequency still healthy (headroom left),
4. Enough data to trust the CPL.

If all four hold: raise budget ~20–30%, re-check in 3–4 days, repeat. Otherwise it's "watch," not
"scale."
